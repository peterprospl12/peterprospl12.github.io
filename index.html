Study Requirements

These are the rules that matter for data quality and downstream consistency.

Target duration

Record a total of 10 minutes. The safest target is 10 complete one-minute segments.

Fixed preset

The recorder stays in NOSE mode and saves 60-second segments automatically, so participants do not have to manage multiple recording modes.

Quiet environment

Avoid TV, music, conversations, fans, traffic noise, or loud keyboard noise in the background.

Practical recommendation

Keep recording continuously until the progress bar reaches 10 minutes. If you need a break, use Pause and then Resume Recording to continue the same segment. Downloading the ZIP will close any unfinished segment.

How It Works

Follow the flow below to keep the submission consistent across participants.

Use a desktop or laptop with a working microphone. A quiet room is strongly preferred.

Record and label live

Stay in NOSE mode, label inhale, exhale, and silence, and continue until you reach 10 minutes.

Download and submit

Download the ZIP package and upload it through the study form linked on this page.

Recording setup and quality

Please use a desktop or laptop with a working external microphone or headset microphone. Recordings made with a built-in laptop microphone, a mobile device microphone, or with substantial background noise may be rejected if the audio quality is not sufficient for evaluation.

Before starting the full session, use the Microphone Check option below to confirm that the correct external or headset microphone is selected and that the audio is clear enough for evaluation.

Mobile devices are not recommended for this task and should not be used unless the study description explicitly allows them.

Labeling reminder

During the recording, remember to label the breathing phases live by clicking the label buttons or using the keyboard shortcuts. Use Inhale when breathing in, Exhale when breathing out, and Silence during pauses or when no clear breath sound should be labeled.

Example: if you start breathing in, click Inhale; when the breath changes to breathing out, click Exhale; if there is a short pause between breaths, click Silence until the next inhale begins.

FAQ

Short answers to the most common participant questions.

Do I need to install anything?

No. The recorder runs directly in the browser. Participants only need to open the page and allow microphone access.

Why can I not change the recording mode?

This study is locked to NOSE mode on purpose. The goal is to keep all participant recordings in the same breathing setup.

What if the microphone does not work?

Refresh the device list, choose another microphone, and make sure the browser has permission to use audio input. If needed, close other apps that may be using the microphone.

Can I use a phone?

A desktop or laptop is recommended. Phones may be less reliable for permissions, shortcuts, and precise live labeling.

What should I enter as the participant name?

Use a participant code only. Do not enter a real name or personal information.

How should I submit the files?

Download the ZIP package from the recorder and upload it through the Google form linked above.
